Continuing the idea of the beloved’s distillation into poetry (in the couplet of s. <a class="rank-math-link" href="https://shakespeare.folger.edu/shakespeares-works/shakespeares-sonnets/sonnet-54/">54</a>), the poet now claims that his verse will be a “living record” in which the beloved will “shine . . . bright” until Doomsday.
