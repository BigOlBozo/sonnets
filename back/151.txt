<br/><a name="Son-151-01"></a>Love is too young to know what conscience is;<br/><a name="Son-151-02"></a>Yet who knows not conscience is born of love?<br/><a name="Son-151-03"></a>Then, gentle cheater, urge not my amiss,<br/><a name="Son-151-04"></a>Lest guilty of my faults thy sweet self prove.<br/><a name="Son-151-05"></a>For, thou betraying me, I do betray<br/><a name="Son-151-06"></a>My nobler part to my gross body’s treason.<br/><a name="Son-151-07"></a>My soul doth tell my body that he may<br/><a name="Son-151-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-151-08">8</span>Triumph in love; flesh stays no farther reason,<br/><a name="Son-151-09"></a>But, rising at thy name, doth point out thee<br/><a name="Son-151-10"></a>As his triumphant prize. Proud of this pride,<br/><a name="Son-151-11"></a>He is contented thy span class="lineNbr" name="Son-151-12">12</span>To stand in thy affairs, fall by thy side.<br/><a name="Son-151-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>No want of conscience hold it that I call<br/><a name="Son-151-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>Her “love,” for whose dear love I rise and fall.</div>
