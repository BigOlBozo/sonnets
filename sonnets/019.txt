<br/><a name="Son-019-01"></a>Devouring Time, blunt thou the lion’s paws<br/><a name="Son-019-02"></a>And make the Earth devour her own sweet brood;<br/><a name="Son-019-03"></a>Pluck the keen teeth from the fierce tiger’s <span class="half-square">⌜</span>jaws,<span class="half-square">⌝</span><br/><a name="Son-019-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-019-04">4</span>And burn the long-lived phoenix in her blood;<br/><a name="Son-019-05"></a>Make glad and sorry seasons as thou fleet’st<br/><a name="Son-019-06"></a>And do whate’er thou wilt, swift-footed Time,<br/><a name="Son-019-07"></a>To the wide world and all her fading sweets.<br/><a name="Son-019-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-019-08">8</span>But I forbid thee one most heinous crime:<br/><a name="Son-019-09"></a>O, carve not with thy hours my love’s fair brow,<br/><a name="Son-019-10"></a>Nor draw no lines there with thine antique pen;<br/><a name="Son-019-11"></a>Him in thy course untainted do allow<br/><a name="Son-019-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-019-12">12</span>For beauty’s pattern to succeeding men.<br/><a name="Son-019-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>Yet do thy worst, old Time; despite thy wrong,<br/><a name="Son-019-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>My love shall in my verse ever live young.</div>
