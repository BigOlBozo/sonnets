<br/><a name="Son-081-01"></a>Or I shall live your epitaph to make<br/><a name="Son-081-02"></a>Or you survive when I in earth am rotten.<br/><a name="Son-081-03"></a>From hence your memory death cannot take,<br/><a name="Son-081-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-081-04">4</span>Although in me each part will be forgotten.<br/><a name="Son-081-05"></a>Your name from hence immortal life shall have,<br/><a name="Son-081-06"></a>Though I, once gone, to all the world must die.<br/><a name="Son-081-07"></a>The Earth can yield me but a common grave,<br/><a name="Son-081-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-081-08">8</span>When you entombèd in men’s eyes shall lie.<br/><a name="Son-081-09"></a>Your monument shall be my gentle verse,<br/><a name="Son-081-10"></a>Which eyes not yet created shall o’erread;<br/><a name="Son-081-11"></a>And tongues to be your being shall rehearse<br/><a name="Son-081-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-081-12">12</span>When all the breathers of this world are dead.<br/><a name="Son-081-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>You still shall live—such virtue hath my pen—<br/><a name="Son-081-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>Where breath most breathes, even in the mouths of men.</div>
