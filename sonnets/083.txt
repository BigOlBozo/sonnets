<br/><a name="Son-083-01"></a>I never saw that you did painting need<br/><a name="Son-083-02"></a>And therefore to your fair no painting set.<br/><a name="Son-083-03"></a>I found, or thought I found, you did exceed<br/><a name="Son-083-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-083-04">4</span>The barren tender of a poet’s debt.<br/><a name="Son-083-05"></a>And therefore have I slept in your report,<br/><a name="Son-083-06"></a>That you yourself, being extant, well might show<br/><a name="Son-083-07"></a>How far a modern quill doth come too short,<br/><a name="Son-083-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-083-08">8</span>Speaking of worth, what worth in you doth grow.<br/><a name="Son-083-09"></a>This silence for my sin you did impute,<br/><a name="Son-083-10"></a>Which shall be most my glory, being dumb,<br/><a name="Son-083-11"></a>For I impair not beauty, being mute,<br/><a name="Son-083-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-083-12">12</span>When others would give life and bring a tomb.<br/><a name="Son-083-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>There lives more life in one of your fair eyes<br/><a name="Son-083-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>Than both your poets can in praise devise.</div>
