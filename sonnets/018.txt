<br/><a name="Son-018-01"></a>Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day?<br/><a name="Son-018-02"></a>Thou art more lovely and more temperate.<br/><a name="Son-018-03"></a>Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May,<br/><a name="Son-018-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-018-04">4</span>And summer’s lease hath all too short a date.<br/><a name="Son-018-05"></a>Sometime too hot the eye of heaven shines,<br/><a name="Son-018-06"></a>And often is his gold complexion dimmed;<br/><a name="Son-018-07"></a>And every fair from fair sometime declines,<br/><a name="Son-018-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-018-08">8</span>By chance or nature’s changing course untrimmed.<br/><a name="Son-018-09"></a>But thy eternal summer shall not fade<br/><a name="Son-018-10"></a>Nor lose possession of that fair thou ow’st,<br/><a name="Son-018-11"></a>Nor shall Death brag thou wand’rest in his shade,<br/><a name="Son-018-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-018-12">12</span>When in eternal lines to time thou grow’st.<br/><a name="Son-018-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>So long as men can breathe or eyes can see,<br/><a name="Son-018-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.</div>
