b'<div class="div1" id="Son-104">\n<div class="page sceneHeader bold">104</div>\n<br/><a name="Son-104-01"></a>To me, fair friend, you never can be old,<br/><a name="Son-104-02"></a>For as you were when first your eye I eyed,<br/><a name="Son-104-03"></a>Such seems your beauty still. Three winters cold<br/><a name="Son-104-04"></a>Have from the forests shook three summers' pride,<br/><a name="Son-104-05"></a>Three beauteous springs to yellow autumn turned<br/><a name="Son-104-06"></a>In process of the seasons have I seen,<br/><a name="Son-104-07"></a>Three April perfumes in three hot Junes burned,<br/><a name="Son-104-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-104-08">8</span>Since first I saw you fresh, which yet are green.<br/><a name="Son-104-09"></a>Ah, yet doth beauty, like a dial hand,<br/><a name="Son-104-10"></a>Steal from his figure, and no pace perceived;<br/><a name="Son-104-11"></a>So your sweet hue, which methinksspan class="lineNbr" name="Son-104-12">12</span>Hath motion, and mine eye may be deceived.<br/><a name="Son-104-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;">\xc2\xa0</span>For fear of which, hear this, thou age unbred:<br/><a name="Son-104-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;">\xc2\xa0</span>Ere you were born was beauty's summer dead.</div>'
