<br/><a name="Son-037-01"></a>As a decrepit father takes delight<br/><a name="Son-037-02"></a>To see his active child do deeds of youth,<br/><a name="Son-037-03"></a>So I, made lame by fortune’s dearest spite,<br/><a name="Son-037-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-037-04">4</span>Take all my comfort of thy worth and truth.<br/><a name="Son-037-05"></a>For whether beauty, birth, or wealth, or wit,<br/><a name="Son-037-06"></a>Or any of these all, or all, or more,<br/><a name="Son-037-07"></a>Entitled in <span class="half-square">⌜</span>thy<span class="half-square">⌝</span> parts do crownèd sit,<br/><a name="Son-037-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-037-08">8</span>I make my love engrafted to this store.<br/><a name="Son-037-09"></a>So then I am not lame, poor, nor despised<br/><a name="Son-037-10"></a>Whilst that this shadow doth such substance give<br/><a name="Son-037-11"></a>That I in thy abundance am sufficed<br/><a name="Son-037-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-037-12">12</span>And by a part of all thy glory live.<br/><a name="Son-037-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>Look what is best, that best I wish in thee.<br/><a name="Son-037-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>This wish I have, then ten times happy me.</div>
