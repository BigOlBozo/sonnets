<br/><a name="Son-126-01"></a>O thou, my lovely boy, who in thy power<br/><a name="Son-126-02"></a>Dost hold Time’s fickle glass, his sickle hour;<br/><a name="Son-126-03"></a>Who hast by waning grown, and therein show’st<br/><a name="Son-126-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-126-04">4</span>Thy lover’s withering as thy sweet self grow’st.<br/><a name="Son-126-05"></a>If Nature, sovereign mistress over wrack,<br/><a name="Son-126-06"></a>As thou goest onwards still will pluck thee back,<br/><a name="Son-126-07"></a>She keeps thee to this purpose, that her skill<br/><a name="Son-126-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-126-08">8</span>May Time disgrace, and wretched <span class="half-square">⌜</span>minutes<span class="half-square">⌝</span> kill.<br/><a name="Son-126-09"></a>Yet fear her, O thou minion of her pleasure!<br/><a name="Son-126-10"></a>She may detain, but not still keep, her treasure.<br/><a name="Son-126-11"></a>Her audit, though delayed, answered must be,<br/><a name="Son-126-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-126-12">12</span>And her quietus is to render thee.</div>
