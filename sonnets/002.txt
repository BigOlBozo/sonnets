<br/><a name="Son-002-01"></a>When forty winters shall besiege thy brow<br/><a name="Son-002-02"></a>And dig deep trenches in thy beauty’s field,<br/><a name="Son-002-03"></a>Thy youth’s proud livery, so gazed on now,<br/><a name="Son-002-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-002-04">4</span>Will be a tattered weed of small worth held.<br/><a name="Son-002-05"></a>Then being asked where all thy beauty lies,<br/><a name="Son-002-06"></a>Where all the treasure of thy lusty days,<br/><a name="Son-002-07"></a>To say within thine own deep-sunken eyes<br/><a name="Son-002-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-002-08">8</span>Were an all-eating shame and thriftless praise.<br/><a name="Son-002-09"></a>How much more praise deserved thy beauty’s use<br/><a name="Son-002-10"></a>If thou couldst answer “This fair child of mine<br/><a name="Son-002-11"></a>Shall sum my count and make my old excuse,”<br/><a name="Son-002-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-002-12">12</span>Proving his beauty by succession thine.<br/><a name="Son-002-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>This were to be new made when thou art old<br/><a name="Son-002-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>And see thy blood warm when thou feel’st it cold.</div>
