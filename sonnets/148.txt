<br/><a name="Son-148-01"></a>O me, what eyes hath love put in my head,<br/><a name="Son-148-02"></a>Which have no correspondence with true sight!<br/><a name="Son-148-03"></a>Or if they have, where is my judgment fled,<br/><a name="Son-148-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-148-04">4</span>That censures falsely what they see aright?<br/><a name="Son-148-05"></a>If that be fair whereon my false eyes dote,<br/><a name="Son-148-06"></a>What means the world to say it is not so?<br/><a name="Son-148-07"></a>If it be not, then love doth well denote<br/><a name="Son-148-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-148-08">8</span>Love’s eye is not so true as all men’s “no.”<br/><a name="Son-148-09"></a>How can it? O, how can love’s eye be true,<br/><a name="Son-148-10"></a>That is so vexed with watching and with tears?<br/><a name="Son-148-11"></a>No marvel then though I mistake my view;<br/><a name="Son-148-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-148-12">12</span>The sun itself sees not till heaven clears.<br/><a name="Son-148-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>O cunning love, with tears thou keep’st me blind,<br/><a name="Son-148-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>Lest eyes well-seeing thy foul faults should find.</div>
