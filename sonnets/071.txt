<br/><a name="Son-071-01"></a>No longer mourn for me when I am dead<br/><a name="Son-071-02"></a>Than you shall hear the surly sullen bell<br/><a name="Son-071-03"></a>Give warning to the world that I am fled<br/><a name="Son-071-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-071-04">4</span>From this vile world with vilest worms to dwell.<br/><a name="Son-071-05"></a>Nay, if you read this line, remember not<br/><a name="Son-071-06"></a>The hand that writ it, for I love you so<br/><a name="Son-071-07"></a>That I in your sweet thoughts would be forgot,<br/><a name="Son-071-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-071-08">8</span>If thinking on me then should make you woe.<br/><a name="Son-071-09"></a>O, if, I say, you look upon this verse<br/><a name="Son-071-10"></a>When I, perhaps, compounded am with clay,<br/><a name="Son-071-11"></a>Do not so much as my poor name rehearse,<br/><a name="Son-071-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-071-12">12</span>But let your love even with my life decay,<br/><a name="Son-071-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>Lest the wise world should look into your moan<br/><a name="Son-071-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>And mock you with me after I am gone.</div>
