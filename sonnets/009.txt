<br/><a name="Son-009-01"></a>Is it for fear to wet a widow’s eye<br/><a name="Son-009-02"></a>That thou consum’st thyself in single life?<br/><a name="Son-009-03"></a>Ah, if thou issueless shalt hap to die,<br/><a name="Son-009-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-009-04">4</span>The world will wail thee like a makeless wife;<br/><a name="Son-009-05"></a>The world will be thy widow and still weep<br/><a name="Son-009-06"></a>That thou no form of thee hast left behind,<br/><a name="Son-009-07"></a>When every private widow well may keep,<br/><a name="Son-009-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-009-08">8</span>By children’s eyes, her husband’s shape in mind.<br/><a name="Son-009-09"></a>Look what an unthrift in the world doth spend<br/><a name="Son-009-10"></a>Shifts but his place, for still the world enjoys it;<br/><a name="Son-009-11"></a>But beauty’s waste hath in the world an end,<br/><a name="Son-009-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-009-12">12</span>And, kept unused, the user so destroys it.<br/><a name="Son-009-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>No love toward others in that bosom sits<br/><a name="Son-009-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>That on himself such murd’rous shame commits.</div>
