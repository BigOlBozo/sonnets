<br/><a name="Son-098-01"></a>From you have I been absent in the spring,<br/><a name="Son-098-02"></a>When proud-pied April, dressed in all his trim,<br/><a name="Son-098-03"></a>Hath put a spirit of youth in everything,<br/><a name="Son-098-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-098-04">4</span>That heavy Saturn laughed and leapt with him.<br/><a name="Son-098-05"></a>Yet nor the lays of birds nor the sweet smell<br/><a name="Son-098-06"></a>Of different flowers in odor and in hue<br/><a name="Son-098-07"></a>Could make me any summer’s story tell,<br/><a name="Son-098-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-098-08">8</span>Or from their proud lap pluck them where they grew.<br/><a name="Son-098-09"></a>Nor did I wonder at the lily’s white,<br/><a name="Son-098-10"></a>Nor praise the deep vermilion in the rose;<br/><a name="Son-098-11"></a>They were but sweet, but figures of delight,<br/><a name="Son-098-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-098-12">12</span>Drawn after you, you pattern of all those.<br/><a name="Son-098-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>Yet seemed it winter still, and, you away,<br/><a name="Son-098-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>As with your shadow I with these did play.</div>
