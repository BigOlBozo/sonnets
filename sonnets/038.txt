<br/><a name="Son-038-01"></a>How can my muse want subject to invent<br/><a name="Son-038-02"></a>While thou dost breathe that pour’st into my verse<br/><a name="Son-038-03"></a>Thine own sweet argument, too excellent<br/><a name="Son-038-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-038-04">4</span>For every vulgar paper to rehearse?<br/><a name="Son-038-05"></a>O, give thyself the thanks if aught in me<br/><a name="Son-038-06"></a>Worthy perusal stand against thy sight,<br/><a name="Son-038-07"></a>For who’s so dumb that cannot write to thee<br/><a name="Son-038-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-038-08">8</span>When thou thyself dost give invention light?<br/><a name="Son-038-09"></a>Be thou the tenth muse, ten times more in worth<br/><a name="Son-038-10"></a>Than those old nine which rhymers invocate;<br/><a name="Son-038-11"></a>And he that calls on thee, let him bring forth<br/><a name="Son-038-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-038-12">12</span>Eternal numbers to outlive long date.<br/><a name="Son-038-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>If my slight muse do please these curious days,<br/><a name="Son-038-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>The pain be mine, but thine shall be the praise.</div>
