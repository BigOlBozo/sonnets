<br/><a name="Son-017-01"></a>Who will believe my verse in time to come<br/><a name="Son-017-02"></a>If it were filled with your most high deserts?<br/><a name="Son-017-03"></a>Though yet, heaven knows, it is but as a tomb<br/><a name="Son-017-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-017-04">4</span>Which hides your life and shows not half your parts.<br/><a name="Son-017-05"></a>If I could write the beauty of your eyes<br/><a name="Son-017-06"></a>And in fresh numbers number all your graces,<br/><a name="Son-017-07"></a>The age to come would say “This poet lies;<br/><a name="Son-017-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-017-08">8</span>Such heavenly touches ne’er touched earthly faces.”<br/><a name="Son-017-09"></a>So should my papers, yellowed with their age,<br/><a name="Son-017-10"></a>Be scorned, like old men of less truth than tongue,<br/><a name="Son-017-11"></a>And your true rights be termed a poet’s rage<br/><a name="Son-017-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-017-12">12</span>And stretchèd meter of an antique song.<br/><a name="Son-017-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>But were some child of yours alive that time,<br/><a name="Son-017-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>You should live twice—in it and in my rhyme.</div>
