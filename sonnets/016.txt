<br/><a name="Son-016-01"></a>But wherefore do not you a mightier way<br/><a name="Son-016-02"></a>Make war upon this bloody tyrant Time,<br/><a name="Son-016-03"></a>And fortify yourself in your decay<br/><a name="Son-016-04"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-016-04">4</span>With means more blessèd than my barren rhyme?<br/><a name="Son-016-05"></a>Now stand you on the top of happy hours,<br/><a name="Son-016-06"></a>And many maiden gardens, yet unset,<br/><a name="Son-016-07"></a>With virtuous wish would bear your living flowers,<br/><a name="Son-016-08"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-016-08">8</span>Much liker than your painted counterfeit.<br/><a name="Son-016-09"></a>So should the lines of life that life repair<br/><a name="Son-016-10"></a>Which this time’s pencil or my pupil pen<br/><a name="Son-016-11"></a>Neither in inward worth nor outward fair<br/><a name="Son-016-12"></a><span class="lineNbr" name="Son-016-12">12</span>Can make you live yourself in eyes of men.<br/><a name="Son-016-13"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>To give away yourself keeps yourself still,<br/><a name="Son-016-14"></a><span class="alignment" style="padding-left:10px;"> </span>And you must live, drawn by your own sweet skill.</div>
