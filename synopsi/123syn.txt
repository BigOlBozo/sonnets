The poet repeats an idea from s. <a class="rank-math-link" href="https://shakespeare.folger.edu/shakespeares-works/shakespeares-sonnets/sonnet-59/">59</a>—that there is nothing new under the sun—and accuses Time of tricking us into perceiving things as new only because we live for such a short time. He reasserts his vow to remain constant despite Time’s power.
